Overview

Designing a climate-tech brand that earns trust on a global stage.

Angawatch is a flood early-warning system and cognitive community platform built to protect lives and livelihoods in flood-prone regions of Kenya. I co-founded the venture and lead all visual design — from the initial logo through to the pitch materials used at international competitions.

The design challenge was significant: we needed a brand that felt credible to both local communities we serve and international judges, investors, and tech organisations evaluating our work. Too polished and it would feel disconnected from the ground reality; too rough and it would undermine our credibility on the international circuit.

Red Bull Basement Kenya — Top 15 / 3,800+ entrants ICASSP International — Top 5 Startup IEEE Climate Hackathon — Award Winner

Brand Identity

An identity built for both the village and the conference hall.

The Angawatch logomark abstracts a water ripple and a watchful eye — referencing both the flood-warning mission and the community monitoring function of the platform. Deep ocean blue communicates authority and trust, while a sharp sky-blue accent signals the technology layer and immediacy of the alert system.

The identity system was built to be legible at every scale: from a 2-inch business card to a full-screen pitch deck slide projected in a competition arena. Icon usage, typography rules, and colour ratios were documented in a brand guide used by both team members and partner organisations.

Pitch Design

Pitch decks that secured recognition across three competitions.

The Angawatch pitch deck was a living document — redesigned and refined before each competition, tailored to different audiences: community development organisations, IEEE climate engineers, and Red Bull's startup ecosystem. Each version maintained brand consistency while foregrounding the metrics and narrative that mattered most to each specific judging panel.

Visual storytelling was central to the pitch: infographics showing flood risk zones, simplified system architecture diagrams, and before/after community impact frames helped non-technical judges grasp the real-world stakes of early flood warning.
