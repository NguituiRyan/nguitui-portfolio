Overview

Designing for the creator economy means designing for the split-second.

FlossyTruKid is a Kenyan content creator building an audience across YouTube and social platforms — someone whose growth depends on the immediate impact of their visual presence. In the creator economy, a thumbnail has under 200 milliseconds to convince a viewer to click. A poster must convey personality, credibility, and context before the eye even settles on the text.

I was brought in to elevate the visual quality across both business-facing and audience-facing creative: promotional business posters for partnerships and brand collaborations, and a YouTube thumbnail system that could consistently outperform competing content in a crowded feed.

Business Posters

Credibility for a creator who means business.

When a creator approaches a brand for a partnership or collaboration, their media kit and promotional materials are the first signal of professionalism. FlossyTruKid's business-facing posters needed to signal that this was a creator who took their brand seriously — not just another face with a phone camera.

The poster series used a high-contrast magenta and deep purple palette that stands out in both digital and physical contexts — memorable without being garish. Custom photo treatments were applied to source imagery to create a consistent, cinematic look across all pieces, creating visual unity even when the content varied significantly.

YouTube Thumbnails

Thumbnails engineered to get clicked.

YouTube thumbnail design is one of the most performance-sensitive disciplines in graphic design — every element is optimised for click-through rate. I developed a thumbnail system for FlossyTruKid's channel that balanced brand consistency with the flexibility to adapt to different video topics and emotional registers.

The system used three core components: a dominant face crop with strong eye contact, a bold 2–4 word headline in high-contrast display type, and a colour-coded background that varied by content category while staying within the overall brand palette. The result was a channel grid that looked intentional and professional.
