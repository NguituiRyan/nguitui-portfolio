Overview

Engineering students deserve engineering-grade event design.

SCESA — the Strathmore Computing & Engineering Students Association — hosts a regular calendar of events: team-building days, hackathons, panel discussions, and industry networking sessions. As the designer embedded within the Strathmore community, I was the natural choice to handle their event creative and on-the-ground content capture.

The work spanned both digital and physical: poster designs created and distributed to noticeboards and WhatsApp groups ahead of events, plus live shooting and post-production editing of video content published on the association's social channels.

Video

On-the-ground content that brings the energy to screen.

Event photography and videography at Strathmore is fast-moving — rooms fill quickly, moments are unrepeatable, and the content needs to land on social media while the energy is still live. I shot and edited video content for SCESA events using my Samsung S23 Ultra and DJI microphone setup, delivering edited cuts within hours of the event wrapping.

The editing style prioritised authenticity over polish: genuine reactions, crowd moments, and speaker highlights — the kind of content that makes students who missed the event genuinely regret it, and makes attendees feel proud to have been part of it.
