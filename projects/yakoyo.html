Yakoyo

A promotional poster series for a Nairobi-based hospitality and events brand — bold, warm, and built to stop the scroll.

RoleGraphic Designer
ClientYakoyo
Year2024
DeliverablesPromotional Posters · Social Media Graphics · Print

Overview

Nairobi hospitality deserves design that matches its energy.

Yakoyo is a Nairobi brand operating in the food, events, and hospitality space — an industry where visual presence directly drives footfall and bookings. In a competitive market flooded with generic templates, they needed creative that felt distinctive: warm, inviting, and unmistakably theirs.

I was brought on to design a promotional poster series for their events and seasonal offerings. Each poster needed to communicate the atmosphere of the experience before anyone set foot through the door — the heat of the food, the energy of the room, the quality of the moment they were about to have.

Design Direction

Warmth as a design language.

The Yakoyo poster series was built around a deliberately warm palette — terracotta, burnt orange, deep amber, and natural whites that reference the textures of quality food and convivial spaces. Typography was set large and confident: the kind of headline-first layouts that read in a single glance on a WhatsApp forward or a pinned noticeboard.

Compositionally, each poster followed a clear visual hierarchy: the occasion headline dominates, supporting detail is secondary, and the brand identity is consistent without overwhelming the event-specific messaging. This allowed the series to feel cohesive across multiple releases while each individual piece still felt crafted for its specific occasion.

Format & Distribution

Optimised for every surface — screen and print.

Each poster in the series was produced in multiple formats: 1080×1080 and 1080×1920 for Instagram and WhatsApp story distribution, and full A2 and A3 print-ready exports for physical notice boards, venue entrances, and event flier drops. The colour profiles were CMYK-calibrated to ensure consistency between the vibrant screen versions and the printed output.

The designs were delivered as layered source files, enabling Yakoyo's team to swap event details for future dates without losing design consistency — building a sustainable system rather than a one-time deliverable.
