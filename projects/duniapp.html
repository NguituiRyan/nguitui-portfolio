Branding · Video

DuniApp

Brand identity and full TikTok video production for a Common App mentorship platform helping Kenyan students access top global universities.

RoleCo-founder · Brand & Video Lead
VentureDuniApp
Year2024 – 2025
DeliverablesBrand Identity · TikTok Videos · Motion Graphics

Overview

Making global university access feel possible — and exciting.

DuniApp was a mentorship platform co-founded to close the information gap for Kenyan students applying to US, UK, and international universities through the Common Application. Most students navigate this process with little guidance — DuniApp paired them with mentor alumni who had walked the same path.

As co-founder and design lead, I was responsible for the brand's entire visual system and all video content production. The challenge was building a brand that felt both aspirational — global, polished, ambitious — and genuinely relatable to Kenyan high school students who hadn't yet seen themselves as Ivy League material.

Brand Identity

A brand that speaks fluently in both Nairobi and New Haven.

The DuniApp identity had to work across two very different contexts: local Kenyan schools and social media feeds, and the international startup ecosystem where we pitched for partnerships and funding. The logomark combined a globe motif with a subtle upward arrow — progression, global reach, and the act of lifting students toward opportunity.

The warm amber-and-gold colour palette was deliberate: we wanted warmth and optimism rather than the cold blues often associated with academic institutions. Typography was kept modern and approachable — no serif formality, just clean legibility that felt confident without feeling intimidating.

TikTok Video

Content that turned college admissions anxiety into excitement.

DuniApp's primary growth channel was TikTok — the platform where Kenyan students spend the most time and where we could reach them before anyone else had framed the Common App as an opportunity rather than an obstacle. I planned, filmed, and edited the full video content library: explainer videos, mentor spotlights, application tip series, and reaction videos to Common App prompts.

The production approach was intentionally raw and personal — shot on my Samsung S23 Ultra with the DJI mic for clean audio, with minimal motion graphics overlaid to reinforce key facts. The editing style mirrored the fast-cut, caption-heavy format that performs on Kenyan TikTok, keeping watch time high and drop-off low.
